Course Overview

What This Course Is About

AI product managers often need to make decisions about how an AI system should work and what to do when it falls short. Those decisions can affect product quality, user experience, cost, and business sustainability. Without a structured approach, it is easy to rely on intuition instead of evidence.

This module introduces two connected ideas: the core AI architecture approaches—RAG, Fine-Tuning, and Agentic Workflows—and the EVAL framework, a five-stage process for evaluating system performance, identifying root causes, and making more informed product decisions.

By the end of the module, you will understand the key trade-offs behind common AI architecture choices and apply the EVAL framework in a realistic scenario to make a data-informed decision with confidence.

Why This Matters

Building an AI Demo Is Easy. Shipping a Reliable Product Is Not.

Turning a promising prototype into a production AI system requires more than intuition. This module helps product managers make architecture decisions using evidence, evaluation, and cost analysis.

From Demo to Production

Generative AI systems are harder to test and stabilize than traditional software because outputs are non-deterministic. What works in a demo can fail unpredictably at scale.

Beyond Vibe Checks

Subjective testing does not scale. Product managers need rubrics, datasets, and audited evaluation methods to assess AI quality with consistency and confidence.

Diagnose Before You Decide

Evaluation data helps identify the real cause of failure before selecting an architecture. Choosing a solution before understanding the problem is a common and costly mistake.

Cost Matters Too

A strong solution still has to be financially sustainable in production. Architecture choices must be grounded in cost analysis, not just technical performance.

Learning Objectives

What You Will Be Able to Do

By completing both parts of this module, you will be able to:

  1. 01

    Distinguish AI Architecture Options

    Distinguish among RAG, Fine-Tuning, and Agentic Workflows — and know when to use each based on the nature of the problem and the business context.

  2. 02

    Apply the EVAL Framework

    Use the EVAL framework to systematically evaluate the performance of an AI system across all five stages: Setup, Audit, Diagnose, Decide, and Optimize.

  3. 03

    Diagnose Root Causes

    Identify the root cause of AI system failures using structured diagnostic methods — moving beyond surface-level symptoms to understand what is actually breaking and why.

  4. 04

    Compare Solutions Using Quality and Cost Metrics

    Evaluate competing solution options using both quality metrics and cost metrics, including AITCO (AI Total Cost of Ownership), to make defensible architectural decisions.

Framework Preview

The EVAL Framework

Five structured stages that guide you from identifying a problem to making an evidence-based decision.

S

Setup

Define success with a rubric and test dataset.

A

Audit

Verify that the judge is scoring outputs accurately.

D

Diagnose

Find the real cause of failure, not just the obvious symptom.

D

Decide

Choose the best solution by balancing quality, speed, and cost.

O

Optimize

Improve the solution's cost structure and test whether it is sustainable in production.

The full framework is introduced in the video and applied step by step in the interactive simulation.
